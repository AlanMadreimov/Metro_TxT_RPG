My head is buzzing like an overloaded generator. The smell of iodine and blood is in my nose. Flashes of the past:
Grandma is a paramedic, grumbling over a broken knee: "Don't whine - iodine only stings the living."
Night ambulance, fortieth call. My hands are pumping adrenaline on their own while my partner is cursing traffic jams.
The last thing he remembers is a flash outside the window when he was sewing up a cut for a rich client. The thought: "At least I managed to fix the seam..."
Now there's only one thing in my head: "Where's the damn first-aid kit... At least bandages..."
